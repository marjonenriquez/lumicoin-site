About The Atlas

v1.2

Translating biological ledgers into a computable physics engine.

What is it?

A living, mathematically constrained visualizer mapped from stereology and FIB-SEM volumetrics. Every cell in this atlas contains precisely 1,000 distinct particles representing 100% of a given cell's volume budget. A percentage cannot be plugged into the equations that govern physical reality — this atlas translates biological accounting ledgers into a computable, comparative physics engine across tissue architectures.

Who is it for?

Built for students learning cell biology, researchers tracking physical pathology, and curious minds looking to visually decode how cellular identity dictates the physical layout of organelle real estate.

The Origin

My name is Marjon Enriquez — a systems bioenergetics researcher who first put this together to see proportions of mitochondria across cell types based on the most recent evidence. Then I expanded it to the nucleus. Then I thought, why not expand it to all organelles! Hence this atlas.
